I chose to study economics and international relations because I wanted to understand how the world operates. I soon realized that traditional models often fall short, which led me to explore the behavioral foundations of both fields. This sparked my fascination with how individual and group behaviors influence complex outcomes at local, national, and international levels.

Today, I remain deeply curious about how interdisciplinary evidence and rigorous evaluation can enhance both policies and the policymaking process. I am particularly interested not only in how to scale effective interventions but also in identifying and addressing the barriers that prevent these insights from being fully understood and applied in practice.
