About

I quickly became fascinated with how economic and international models were theorized without the behavioral factors that create them. Since then, my work has focused on the behavioral, economic, and structural barriers to improving policies and policymaking processes. 

Below are just a few of the things I can’t help but think about when trying to tackle today and tomorrow's policy challenges:

Economics Policy-making Processes (individual and group) Domestic Policy Time Intergenerational
International Policy History Geography Education Policy Labor Policy
Inclusive, Representative, and Adaptive Policies Structures Behaviors Environmental Social Policy
