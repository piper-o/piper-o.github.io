About

My interests involve supporting public servants and institutions in improving decision-making environments and organizational conditions to promote evidence-driven policy.

I earned a B.A. in economics and international relations from Connecticut College, where I completed a behavioral economics honors thesis and received the Chair’s Prize in Economics. I have applied behavioral science experience across academic and policy settings, including roles at Harvard Kennedy School, Duke University, and the MIT Sloan School of Management.

I currently serve as the Behavioral Research Coordinator at Harvard Business School, where I support research exploring individual, group, and organizational decision-making. I have supplemented this professional research work with graduate-level coursework in statistics, data, and public policy at the University of Chicago Harris School of Public Policy and Harvard Extension School.

In 2025, I was selected as one of 29 participants in J-PAL North America’s Research Staff Training and as one of 20 participants in McGill’s Max Bell School of Public Policy Evaluation Capacity Case Challenge.

Outside of work, I enjoy spending time exploring the outdoors and supporting community gardens and local organizations.
