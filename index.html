About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of behavioral science, economics, and public policy. I first studied economics and international relations to better understand how the world operates, but soon realized that traditional models often fall short. This drew me toward the behavioral foundations of decision-making and policy, where I remain deeply curious about how interdisciplinary evidence and rigorous evaluation can strengthen both policies and the policymaking process.

My current research interests include the economic, structural, and behavioral barriers that prevent evidence from being adopted or scaled, and how history and geography intertwine with those forces to shape today’s policy challenges. More broadly, I am committed to bridging the gap between rigorous research and practical policymaking.

Outside of research, I love watercolor painting, swimming, puzzles, gardening (both food and flowers), and spending time in nature.
