I chose to study economics and international relations because I wanted to understand how the world operates. I soon realized that traditional models often fall short, which led me to explore the behavioral foundations of both fields. This ignited my fascination with how individual and group behaviors influence complex outcomes at local, national, and international levels.

Today, I remain deeply curious about how interdisciplinary evidence and rigorous evaluations can enhance both policies and the policymaking process. I am particularly interested in not only scaling interventions but also identifying and addressing the barriers that hinder the understanding and practical application of these insights.
