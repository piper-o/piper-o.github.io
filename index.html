About

I'm currently focused on other projects and coursework at the Harvard Extension School (Intermediate Statistics and Writing in the Social Sciences), so I haven’t had time to give this site the attention it deserves. I’ll return to it when exams are over, but in the meantime, please feel free to reach out!

I chose to study economics and international relations because I wanted to understand how the world operates. I soon realized that traditional models often fall short, which led me to explore the behavioral foundations of both fields.

Today, I remain deeply curious about how interdisciplinary evidence and rigorous evaluation can enhance both policies and the policymaking process. I am particularly interested not only in how to scale effective interventions but also in identifying and addressing the barriers that prevent these insights from being fully understood and applied in practice.
