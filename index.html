About

Hello!

I believe in building local and state governance capacity through shaping data-driven policy inputs for action and reforming policymaking environments for learning and adaptation.

When I found the behavioral side of both economics and international relations I was hooked on the potential of research to update these models to more closely fit human behavior and improve policies sets and outcomes. I pursued work in behavioral research labs to study the mechanisms on individual and interpersonal levels to be able to understand how to adjust them for applied field work. I've supplemented my professional experiences with graduate level coursework at the intersection of research and policy and am applying these insights as 1 of 20 members in the 2026 Max Bell School of Public Policy Evaluation Capacity Case Challenge. Now, I am ready to continue on the next phase of my journey in improving policies and policymaking processes through graduate school or more applied policy roles.

If I am not in the library, you can find me exploring the outdoors or supporting community gardens and local nonprofits (some listed below).

Please feel free to reach out! piper.oren(at)gmail(dot)com
